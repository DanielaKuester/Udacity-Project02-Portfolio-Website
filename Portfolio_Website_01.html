Featured Work

  • Mockup to Article

    My first little Udacity project was a mere exercise in HTML. I had to format a pre-written article with the help of HTML. To be honest, I did not really like the "pure" HTML style, so I took a little bit of time to change the font, to add a margin and to justify the text with CSS. The result? The article does not have the style of a website from the 1990s any more - and I like that waaay better. ;) What I like most about the article itself? I think that it is well-written, I like the Gif-image of the Go board with the Udacity logo and I love that it mentions IBM's AI Watson. I was a working student at IBM in the PR department of the "Hochschulprogramme" (German for "University Programs") for nearly one year and I discovered my passion for programming there. I had to research websites that teach teenagers to code - and during the process, I fell in love with coding! :)

  • Animal Trading Card

    The second Udacity project was to create an animal trading card with my favourite animal and to style it with CSS. My favourite animals are cats and tortoises, but I think that squirrels look incredibly cute so I simply couldn't resist - I just had to make my card about squirrels! The animal trading card was a lot of fun! I learned a lot about squirrels and even more about CSS! Fun fact: I was on holidays in Stockholm (Sweden) a few years ago and a little squirrel probably thought that I had hamstered a bit of food. It stood about four inches away from my feet, looked at me and I looked back. I couldn't believe how near it came to me and then suddenly it jumped and held on my t-shirt! (I even have a photo that proves it.) Since that "attack", squirrels also count to my favourite animals.

  • Pixel Art Maker

    The third Udacity project includes my first babysteps in JavaScript. For the Pixel art maker, the task was to program a function that creates a grid with a size that the user can specify. The user can choose a colour with the inbuilt colour-picker and then click a square in the grid to colour it. By double-clicking a square, the colour is removed and reset to white. It made me extremely happy to see that I could write a simple program in JavaScript. I'm planning to update this project in the next few months when my coding abilities grow. I already have some ideas to change the style of the site with CSS and to add a few new functions. So, stay tuned! I will probably also add my future projects to my GitHub account - make sure to check it out! ;)
